An Internet Built For All

The Internet Story

The Internet has transformed our lives and the way we learn, communicate, do business and so much more. Wired and wireless networks keep us constantly connected no matter where we are. Fast and affordable broadband networks are making America a leader in today’s global digital economy.

Most Americans Have
Broadband at Home

Every year, more people are taking advantage of all the opportunities a high-speed internet connection offers. Cable operators are now the largest residential broadband provider in the country. There are still some without broadband, and it is critical we get the remaining people online.

Need for Speed

The more we use the Internet, the more speed we want. Cable networks' ever-increasing speeds fuel all the streaming, downloading and gaming you want. In just the last few years, maximum speeds have quadrupled – now up to 2 Gigabits per second.

Infrastructure and Investment

America's Internet builders have invested $290 Billion of private capital to deploy broadband networks that reach nearly every corner of the U.S.

Cost Reduction

The Price per Mbps has declined from an average of $28.13 in 2000 to $0.64 in 2020.
